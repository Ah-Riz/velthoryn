Your Role

There are 2 developers per team. You share the same codebase and deliver the same product together. The weekly task describes what your dev pair must deliver, not what one person does alone. How you split the work is your decision.

Each developer submits their own weekly report. In your report, you must clearly state:
- What YOU specifically built or contributed this week
- How you and your partner split the work
- Your individual blockers and insights

The reviewer scores each developer individually based on their actual contribution visible through git commits, PRs, and what you describe in your report. If one developer does all the work and the other contributes nothing, the scores will reflect that.

Task Brief
— Stable MVP + Status Report
Context

This is the halfway point. Your BD team is writing their traction report. Your Marketing team is summarizing growth data. You need to make sure the product is stable and write an honest assessment of where things stand.

What Needs to Happen

Fix remaining bugs, ensure the product works reliably end-to-end, and write a status report that your whole team can reference.

Acceptance Criteria

All known bugs from Week 4–7 are fixed or documented as known limitations
Product works end-to-end on devnet without crashes: create stream → view dashboard → withdraw → cancel
Performance check: transaction times, costs, bottlenecks
Status report includes: what's working well, what's not, known bugs, what you'd improve, recommendations for Phase 3
Share status report with your full team — BD and Marketing need to know what the product can and can't do
