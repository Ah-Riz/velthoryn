01   PRODUCT

Everything a token campaign needs.
Nothing it doesn't.

Three primitives — Merkle compression, vesting schedules, and per-recipient clawback — composed into a single Anchor program. No off-chain custody. No multisig theater.

Merkle compression

Distribute to 10,000 wallets with one 32-byte root. Recipients submit proofs, the program verifies on-chain. State stays small, gas stays predictable.

02   HOW IT WORKS

Four moves.
From CSV to claim.

A campaign is a Merkle root plus a vesting program. You upload allocations; recipients claim on their own schedule.

STEP 01

Compress allocations

Drop in a CSV of (address, amount). Velthoryn builds the Merkle tree and stores the 32-byte root on chain.

STEP 02

Set schedule

Pick cliff, linear, or milestone — globally or per recipient. The curve is signed into the campaign config.

STEP 03

Recipients claim

Each wallet submits its Merkle proof. The program verifies, releases the vested portion, marks the leaf.

STEP 04

Rotate or claw back

Mistakes happen. Rotate the root inside the 7-day grace; the next root becomes canonical, unclaimed leaves invalidate.

05   FAQ

Questions, answered.

Everything you might want to know before getting on the waitlist.

01 What is Velthoryn, in one sentence?

A tool for Solana projects to send tokens to many people on a schedule — fairly, automatically, and at a fraction of the usual cost.

02 Who is it for?

Teams paying themselves over years, projects paying investors over months, and communities running airdrops to thousands of supporters.

03 How is it cheaper than just sending tokens?

Instead of writing every recipient to the blockchain (which scales with cost), Velthoryn stores one tiny fingerprint that proves everyone's share. Recipients claim using a short proof. You pay once — not 10,000 times.

04 What if I make a mistake in the list?

You have a 7-day grace window after publishing a campaign. During that window you can update or remove any allocation. After 7 days, the schedule is locked and runs on its own.

05 Does Velthoryn hold my tokens?

No. Tokens stay in a non-custodial Solana program. Velthoryn (the company) cannot touch them — only the rules of your campaign can release them.

06 What schedules can I set?

Four shapes, mix-and-match per recipient: all at once, gradually over time (linear), after a waiting period (cliff), or when specific goals are hit (milestone).

07 When does the product launch?

Public mainnet is targeted for Q3 2026. Waitlist members get early access on devnet first, plus a personal onboarding session for the first 100 teams.

08 Is the code open source?

Yes. The on-chain program and SDKs will be MIT-licensed on GitHub. You can read every line, run your own copy, or fork it. Independent security audits are underway.
